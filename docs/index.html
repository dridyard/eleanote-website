A day with Eleanote

It stays out of the way while the work gets done.

Most of your day is spent in your EHR. Eleanote lives as a small pill at the top of your screen, listening for hotkeys and turning each step of the visit into something you barely have to think about.

Step 01 — Precharting

Highlight, copy, hit a hotkey.

Highlight the recent labs, imaging, and messages in your EHR. Ctrl + C, then F4. Eleanote summarizes what matters so you walk into the room already caught up. Seconds, not minutes.

Hotkey · F4
Step 02 — Seeing the patient

Talk naturally. Stage orders as you go.

One keystroke starts recording. Talk to your patient like you always do. As the plan forms, Eleanote can stage labs, imaging, referrals, and medications in the background so they're ready before the visit ends.

Hotkey · F6
Step 03 — Real-time orders

Imaging, labs, referrals — built while you talk.

You don't stop to type. Eleanote listens and stages the orders you mention, with indications and follow-up timing already attached, ready for your review.

Hotkey · F7
Step 04 — Into the EHR

Note, codes, and orders enter with one keystroke.

Instead of an EHR integration we don't have, Eleanote uses keystroke automations you program. The formatted note, diagnosis codes, and order details land where you want them.

Hotkey · F8
Step 05 — Feedback & learning

Correct it once. It learns for next time.

If a note misses your style, tell it what you want changed. Eleanote rewrites it on the spot and updates your personal profile so the next note already reflects the change.

Hotkey · F9
Step 06 — Other outputs

Patient instructions. Letters. Summaries.

The same system can be trained to produce anything you regularly write — patient instructions, letters to other clinicians, school notes, summaries. Whatever your clinic needs, you can teach it.

What makes it different

Personalization first.
Plug-and-play second.

Most ambient scribes optimize for the average doctor. Eleanote optimizes for you. The whole product is built around a detailed personal profile, frontier-model quality, and automation you can shape.

An ~8,000-token personal profile

Note structure, phrasing, formatting preferences, clinical habits, recurring instructions. Enough room to actually capture how you practice.

Learns every time you correct it

Feedback rewrites the current note immediately and updates your profile, so the next note already reflects the change.

Frontier-model quality

Built on top-tier LLMs with the context window to actually use a long personal profile, instead of forcing every doctor into one template.

Hotkey-driven, not click-driven

You barely look at Eleanote. Programmable keystrokes move notes, codes, orders, and instructions wherever you need them.

Built-in setup chatbots

Customization is powerful, and it does take work. Assistants throughout the program help you shape your profile, hotkeys, and outputs.

Outputs beyond the note

Patient instructions, letters to other clinicians, summaries — anything you regularly write can be trained into a one-keystroke output.

The honest tradeoff

Eleanote does not integrate directly with your EHR.

It can't, and pretending otherwise would be dishonest. Instead it works beside your EHR, using customizable keystroke automations you set up. That means more upfront effort. In exchange you get a scribe that fits your style instead of forcing you into someone else's, and that you can keep tuning forever.

Who it's for

Best for independent-minded doctors
who want the scribe to fit them.

You'll likely love it if…

  • You use a laptop in the exam room.
  • You care a lot about how your notes read.
  • You're tired of click fatigue and repetitive menus.
  • You're willing to invest setup time once for daily payoff.
  • You want a tool that adapts to you, not the other way around.

Probably not for you if…

  • You don't bring a laptop into the exam room.
  • You need a turnkey EHR integration on day one.
  • You'd rather take a generic note than train a personal one.
  • Setting up hotkeys and templates sounds worse than the problem.
