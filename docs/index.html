The honest pitch

More personal than plug-and-play.

Eleanote is for doctors who would rather spend effort training a tool around their own workflow than accept a generic note and a rigid implementation. Each user can develop a detailed personal profile, give feedback on individual notes, and shape the output over time.

That also means the product is best for physicians who are comfortable using a laptop in clinic and willing to customize. The payoff is a scribe that can become much more specific to how you practice.

What it is not

  • Not a direct EHR integration.
  • Not a passive, one-size-fits-all note generator.
  • Not designed around a sales demo first.
  • Not a replacement for physician review or judgment.

Scroll-through workflow mockup

The program stays out of the way while the work gets done.

Most of the day, the physician is looking at the EHR. Eleanote can live as a small pill-shaped assistant at the top of the screen, listening for hotkeys and helping move from encounter context to note, codes, orders, and follow-up materials.

Step 1

Prechart without opening Eleanote

Highlight recent results or notes in the EHR, press Ctrl+C and a hotkey, and Eleanote summarizes what matters immediately before the visit.

Step 2

Talk naturally during the visit

Start recording from the keyboard. You can speak to the patient and also ask the scribe to summarize recent events, results, or the plan.

Step 3

Stage orders while the visit is happening

As the assessment and plan forms, Eleanote can prepare labs, imaging, referrals, medications, indications, and follow-up timing for physician review.

Step 4

Send the work to the EHR with programmed keystrokes

Instead of an API integration, users can train keystroke automations that place formatted notes, diagnosis codes, and order details into the EHR.

Step 5

Correct the note and teach the system

If the note needs improvement, give direct feedback. Eleanote rewrites the note and updates the physician's preference profile for next time.

How Eleanote is different

The advantage is personalization and control.

The product is designed around a detailed physician profile, high-quality language models, and user-directed automation rather than a shallow template or a locked-down EHR integration.

Large personal preference profile

Each physician can develop a detailed profile describing note structure, phrasing, formatting, clinical preferences, and recurring workflow instructions.

Feedback on every note

When a note is wrong, too long, too short, or not in the right style, the physician can correct it and have the system learn from that feedback.

Frontier model quality

Eleanote is built around capable language models with enough context to accommodate individualized instructions rather than forcing everyone into one output style.

Custom keyboard automation

Users can program keystrokes to move notes, formatting, codes, orders, and other generated text into the systems they already use.

Chatbots throughout setup

Built-in assistants help users configure preferences and workflows, because the customization is powerful but does take effort.

Output beyond the note

The same system can be trained to generate patient instructions, letters to other clinicians, summaries, and other clinic-specific materials.

Who it is for

Best for independent-minded doctors who want the tool to adapt to them.

Eleanote is not trying to be the easiest enterprise rollout. It is trying to be a practical, customizable scribe for physicians who want a lot of control and are willing to train the system to match their clinic.

  • Physicians who use a laptop in the exam room.
  • Doctors who care deeply about note style and editing time.
  • Users willing to configure hotkeys and workflows.
  • Clinicians frustrated by repetitive EHR clicking and menu navigation.
  • People who want flexibility more than a locked-down integration.
